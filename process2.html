Poster Project

This is an individual project for IAT102, a course that teaches the basics of graphic design. The premise was to create an advertising exhibition poster for an artist or designer.

Thought Process

I chose to design a poster for Spanish designer Patricia Urquiola, who incorporates the style of minimalism in a playful matter, which suits my interest in creating simple designs.

I started off by sketching different layouts of the poster. The shapes of the placement pictures were sketched in a way for the viewer to not see the full picture, making the viewer curious to see more of the design by going to the exhibit.

Draft Posters

I then chose two sketches that I liked the most and worked on two drafts in Adobe InDesign. The first design was based on Urquiola’s design of shimmering glass products. The second design was based on her product of large ceramics. I mostly played with the typography for both drafts, as I wanted the words to stand out.

Challenges

When I got feedback from my drafts, both posters had issues with the colour palette; the first draft was too bright, making it hard to see, while the second draft was too dull, which makes it look unappealing. My final poster was based on the second draft, but I had trouble changing the colour palette since those products have dark tones. Thus, I decided to start fresh and find another product of Urquiola’s with an appealing colour scheme to work with.

Reflection

My final design was praised for the asymmetry, typography, and colour choice. The only issue was the overuse of bars which are used for highlighting only one piece of information. Overall, I learned that feedback and critiques are important for designers and that it is okay to restart a project if one of your drafts doesn’t meet expectations.
