Graphic Short Story

This is an individual project for IAT100, a course that teaches the basics of visual design and animation. The premise was to create a comic based on a poem or a piece of literature.

Thought Process

I choose to use the poem “A Boat Beneath a Sunny Sky” by Lewis Carroll. It recounts how in the summer, he would have boat rides with Alice Liddell, the inspiration for Alice in Wonderland, and still reminisce these moments even when summer is over. Thus, my take on the poem is about a father and a daughter, with the father grieving, rather than reminiscing, over what happened one summer.

Sketching

I sketched out the main characters to get a clear vision of what they will look like, as well as some small panel layouts and scenes that I might use for the comic.

After, I scanned my sketches to my computer and created a rough page layout with the image editor GIMP. Then, I moved over to Autodesk Sketchbook to draw the outlines of my scenes and panels.

Challenges

By the time I finished the outlines, the deadline of the project was approaching. Due to the page size (8.3” x 11.7”), outlining the panels and drawings took too much of my time. Thus, I decided to colour the comic in black and white, only colouring in what is important; it also fits the sad twist of the story.

Reflection

When I presented my comic, I was complimented by how well drawn it is and that the transitions between panels were easy to follow. Overall, I learned how much work it takes to draw a comic but I enjoyed the final result I had in the end. Even though I wasn’t able to fully colour the scenes, I felt the monochrome tones really suited the story.
